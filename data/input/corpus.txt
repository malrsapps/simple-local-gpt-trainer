Simple Local GPT Trainer is a small educational project.

It shows how a tiny GPT-style model can be trained from a plain text file.
The goal is not to create a powerful language model.
The goal is to make the training process easier to understand.

A user can place text into the data folder.
The preparation script trains a small tokenizer.
The training script builds a tiny transformer model.
The inference script generates text from a prompt.

Small models can learn patterns from small datasets.
They may repeat phrases or memorize the training text.
This behavior is expected in tiny experiments.

Local AI experiments are useful for learning.
They help users understand tokenization, context length, model size, and training steps.
